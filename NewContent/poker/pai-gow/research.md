## Gameplay Mechanics

### Official Rules and Card Mechanics
Pai Gow Poker is played with a standard 52-card deck plus one joker. The game consists of seven cards dealt to each player[1]. Players aim to create two poker hands: a five-card "high" hand and a two-card "low" hand. The "high" hand must outrank the "low" hand. The joker can be used to complete a straight, flush, or as an ace[2].

### Hand Rankings and Dealer Interaction
Hand rankings in Pai Gow Poker follow standard poker rules, except the "wheel" hand (A-2-3-4-5) is the second-highest straight. Players compete against the dealer, winning if both their hands beat the dealer's corresponding hands. If one hand wins and the other loses, it's a push; both must win to collect winnings[3].

### Variations and Updates
There are variations like Fortune Pai Gow, which offers bonus payouts for specific hands, and Emperor's Challenge, which bets on the chance of a seven-card straight flush. Licensing and platform updates have broadened the accessibility of these variations to more players globally[4].
