# Six-Plus (Short Deck) Poker: A Comprehensive Guide

## Introduction to Six-Plus Poker

Six-Plus Poker, also known as Short Deck Poker, is a popular variation of Texas Hold'em that has gained traction worldwide due to its dynamic gameplay and higher action potential. Originating in Asia and quickly spreading to other regions, this variant involves a 36-card deck, omitting all cards from two through five. The removal of these cards leads to altered hand rankings and strategies compared to the traditional Texas Hold'em setup.

## Rules and Differences from Texas Hold’em

### Deck Composition
- **Deck**: Unlike Texas Hold'em’s 52 cards, Six-Plus uses 36 cards, removing the 2s through 5s.

### Hand Rankings
- **Flush beats Full House**: Due to a reduced deck size, making a flush becomes more difficult, hence it outranks a full house.
- **Aces as High or Low**: Aces still function as high or low in straights, with A-6-7-8-9 as the lowest straight.

### Game Play
- **Opening bet mechanics** can vary, but generally follow standard poker rules with antes or blinds.
- **Increased Probabilities**: With fewer cards, the likelihood of landing premium hands like flushes and full houses increases, altering strategic considerations.

## Strategies for Six-Plus Poker

### Aggressive Play Style
- **Increased Variance**: The smaller deck size promotes a more aggressive play style with frequent bluffs and larger pot sizes.

### Hand Selection
- **Strong Pre-flop Hands**: Focus on high pairs and strong connectors, given the increased odds of hitting premium hands post-flop.

### Position Importance
- Positional play remains critical. A solid understanding of opponents’ tendencies paired with aggressive strategies can leverage position better than in traditional Hold'em.
