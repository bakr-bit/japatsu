## Introduction to Omaha Poker

Omaha Poker is a popular variant of poker, known for its strategic depth and complexity. It is available in several formats, including Omaha Hi (or Pot Limit Omaha - PLO), Omaha Hi/Lo, and occasionally, No Limit Omaha. Here's an overview:

### Omaha Hi/Lo

- **Objective**: Players aim to form the best high hand and the best low hand.
- **Rules**: Each player is dealt four hole cards. Using exactly two hole cards combined with three from the community cards, players form their best hand.
- **Popularity**: This variant is less popular than PLO but offers strategic diversity.

### Pot Limit Omaha (PLO)

- **Objective**: Form the best five-card hand.
- **Rules**: Players receive four hole cards and must use two of these with three community cards. Betting is limited to the pot size.
- **Trend**: Remains highly favored, particularly among seasoned players due to its balance of bluffing and probability.

### No Limit Omaha

- **Gambling Style**: Similar to No Limit Hold’em, players can bet any amount up to their entire stack.
- **Availability**: Less common than PLO, catering to high-stakes enthusiasts.
