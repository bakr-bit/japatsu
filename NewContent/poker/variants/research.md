## Overview of Major Poker Variants

Poker games have a wide variety of formats, each with unique gameplay and strategic components. Here’s an exploration of the most common poker variants accessible online, including specifics about their play mechanics and strategic complexities:

1. **Texas Hold'em**: Known for its simplicity and depth, Texas Hold'em involves two hole cards and five community cards. Betting occurs in four rounds, and strategic complexity arises from the need to predict opponents' hands.

2. **Omaha (and Omaha Hi-Lo)**: Similar to Texas Hold'em but with four hole cards. Omaha Hi-Lo adds a split-pot element, rewarding both the highest and lowest hands.

3. **Seven Card Stud**: Involves seven cards dealt to each player, but only the best five-card hand plays. With no community cards, strategic play focuses on memory and observation.

4. **Five Card Draw**: This variant is similar to classic poker, where players are dealt five cards and have the opportunity to exchange some before final betting.

5. **Caribbean Stud**: Players compete against the house rather than each other, requiring a basic understanding of poker hands with no bluffing involved.

6. **Three Card Poker and Ultimate Texas Hold'em**: Both focus on simpler gameplay and faster rounds, appealing to new or casual players.

7. **Video Poker**: An RNG-based format mimicking slot machines, offering various poker hand pays without needing a dealer.
